Selection of applicable methodologies

We have selected the Berkus method for evaluating pre-revenue startups as it emphasizes qualitative factors and assigns financial estimates to reduce the uncertainties associated with early-stage ventures.
We have selected the Risk Factor Summation Method to comprehensively evaluate and solidify the various risks associated with a startup. Unlike the Scorecard Method, which primarily compares a startup to industry benchmarks, this approach enables detailed adjustments based on specific risk categories, offering a more nuanced perspective on startup valuation.
We have selected the Scorecard Method because it compares startups to industry benchmarks, offering a structured approach to evaluate key factors such as team, market, product, and competitive risk, thereby ensuring a balanced and realistic valuation.
We have selected the VC Method because it focuses on future exit value and return on investment, making it particularly suited for investors evaluating high-growth startups with uncertain cash flows.
We have selected the Cost to Duplicate Method because it estimates a startup's value based on the cost to duplicate its assets, offering a straightforward and objective baseline for valuation.
